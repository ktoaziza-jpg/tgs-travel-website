Destination Guide

Turkmenistan

The Marble Oasis & Mystical Gates of Karakum

Step into Central Asia's most exclusive and enigmatic destination. Turkmenistan seamlessly weaves the ancient history of legendary Silk Road fortresses with futuristic grand white-marble avenues and the raw, unforgettable elemental power of the burning Darvaza crater deep within the desert.

Good to Know

Quick Facts

Currency

Turkmen Manat (TMT). Credit cards are practically not accepted; carrying cash is strictly mandatory throughout the trip.

Language

Turkmen (official) and Russian are universally spoken. Our specialized team guides speak fluent English.

Time Zone

TMT (UTC+5). Perfectly mirrors neighboring countries, facilitating smooth multi-Stan planning.

Electricity

220V, 50Hz. Standard European C and F sockets. Strongly recommend high-capacity power banks for desert campnights.

Must-Visit Places

Top Destinations

Holding the Guinness World Record for the highest concentration of white marble architecture, Ashgabat is a surreal, futuristic city of golden domes, massive palaces, and perfectly manicured avenues.

  • Independence Monument: A towering, majestic column surrounded by grand statues of historic Turkmen rulers.
  • The Wedding Palace: A spectacular geometric structure framing a massive golden globe of Turkmenistan.
  • Gypjak Mosque: One of the largest and most magnificent single-dome mosques in all of Central Asia.

Deep inside the remote Karakum desert lies a mesmerizing, 70-meter wide burning gas crater that has been flaming continuously for over fifty years. Watching it glow under the desert stars is unforgettable.

  • Desert Camping: Spend a cozy night in traditional yurt setups directly overlooking the glowing depths.
  • Sunset Transformation: Watch the desert sands slowly turn into dark profiles against the burning orange light.
  • Off-Road Expedition: Reach the area through adrenaline-filled rides across wild sand dunes in heavy SUVs.

Once acclaimed as one of the largest and most populated cosmopolitan urban centers in the ancient world, this vast UNESCO World Heritage site displays millennia of history layered in mud-brick fortresses.

  • Sultan Sanjar Mausoleum: A magnificent 12th-century engineering masterpiece with an incredibly advanced double-dome structure.
  • Greater Kyz Kala: Unique, pleated clay walls of an ancient defensive castle fortress.
  • Historic Archaeological Nodes: Explore five distinct eras of city walls tracking Persian, Islamic, and Mongol paths.
Visa & Entry

Navigating the Letter of Invitation

Turkmenistan maintains some of the most strict entry rules globally. All foreign citizens must obtain an official Letter of Invitation (LOI) approved directly by the State Migration Service before applying for a visa.

We handle the stress: TGS Travel completely manages your entire LOI application process. Once we secure your letter, you can easily receive your physical visa stamp directly upon arriving at Ashgabat Airport or land borders.

Please note that individual, non-escorted tourist travel is not permitted by state law — you must stay with an accredited guide from our company throughout your stay.

Climate Guide

When to Go

The Karakum desert creates extreme temperature shifts. Avoid intense summer highs by choosing pleasant transition windows.

Perfect Desert Windows 🌸

April and May offer beautiful desert conditions. Temperatures are pleasantly warm, spring steppe flowers briefly cover the sands, and city tours are highly comfortable.

Avg. Daytime Temp20–30°C
Desert EveningsPleasant & Cool
Best ForAshgabat & Crater Stays

Extreme Desert Heat 🔥

July and August feature intense heat inside the desert, with temperatures regularly crossing 45°C. Tours are adjusted for early morning or late evening excursions with climate-controlled 4x4 cabins.

Avg. Daytime Temp40–46°C
Sun IntensityVery High
PrecipitationAbsolute zero

Golden Travel Months 🍂

September and October represent another prime window alongside spring. The desert heat completely fades away, leaving perfectly crisp clear days and ideal camping nights at Darvaza.

Avg. Daytime Temp22–32°C
VisibilityExcellent
Best ForAll-Country Multi-Stan Tours

Chilly Winds & Desert Frost ❄️

Winters can get surprisingly cold and windy due to arctic winds hitting the open flat terrain. Desert camping is heavily restricted during this time, but urban monuments remain accessible.
