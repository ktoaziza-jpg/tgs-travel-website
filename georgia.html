Destination Guide

Georgia

The Crown Jewel of the Caucasus

Welcome to Georgia, a breathtakingly beautiful land of dramatic alpine ridges, ancient watchtowers, and legendary hospitality. From the lively terrace cafes of historic Tbilisi to the high-altitude cloud sanctuary of Kazbegi and the 8,000-year-old traditional winemaking heritage of Kakheti, Georgia delivers an unforgettable sensory journey.

Good to Know

Quick Facts

Currency

Georgian Lari (GEL). Contactless credit cards are widely accepted everywhere in major cities and boutique hotels.

Language

Georgian (official language). English and Russian are universally spoken across premier hospitality nodes.

Time Zone

GET (UTC+4). Ideal positioning for seamless flight connectivity from both Europe and Asia hubs.

Electricity

220V, 50Hz. Standard European C and F sockets are fully universal in all guest rooms.

Must-Visit Places

Top Destinations

The vibrant capital tucked neatly into the Mtkvari River valley. Tbilisi showcases a unique charm of carved wooden balconies, ancient brick domes, and a thriving modern art scene.

  • Old Tbilisi & Narikala: Stroll through winding alleys toward the ancient fortress commanding panoramic city views.
  • Abanotubani Sulphur Baths: Historic brick-domed underground thermal bathhouses praised for centuries.
  • The Bridge of Peace: A striking, futuristic glass-and-steel canopy connecting traditional and modern quarters.

A high-altitude alpine dream along the majestic Military Georgian Highway, framed by the sheer vertical rock wall of Mount Kazbek rising above 5,000 meters.

  • Gergeti Trinity Church: The iconic 14th-century stone church standing completely isolated against massive glaciers.
  • Gveleti Waterfalls: Crisp, thunderous mountain streams hidden inside dramatic rocky canyons.
  • Luxury Mountain Lodges: Unwind on panoramic sundecks overlooking snowbound peaks with premium local hospitality.

Acclaimed by UNESCO as the birthplace of traditional winemaking, the sprawling Alazani Valley is lined with premium estates and historic Italianate towns.

  • Sighnaghi (Town of Love): A deeply romantic walled town featuring cobblestone streets and stunning views of the Caucasus.
  • Qvevri Winemaking: Tour ancient royal cellars utilizing traditional buried clay vessels for organic aging.
  • Tsinandali Estate: A magnificent 19th-century aristocratic palace set within beautiful botanical gardens.
Visa & Entry

The Ultimate One-Year Visa Freedom

Georgia operates one of the most open, progressive entry frameworks globally. Citizens of over 95 countries — including the European Union, USA, GCC, UK, and Uzbekistan — can enter completely visa-free for a full 365-day stay.

No advance registration portals or paper submittals are required. Simply arrive with a valid international passport at any land or air border checkpoint smoothly.

Climate Guide

When to Go

From pleasant sub-tropical shorelines to snowy alpine peaks, Georgia transitions beautifully across the calendar.

Blooming Valleys & Easter Magic 🌸

Spring wakes up the lowlands brilliantly. Tbilisi’s courtyard cafes overflow with fresh energy, wildflowers cover the foothills, and Orthodox Easter fills historic stone cathedrals with grand choral chants.

Avg. Lowlands Temp15–23°C
Mountain PassesClearing smoothly
Best ForTbilisi Exploration & Mtskheta Heritage Walks

High-Altitude Alpine Treks ☀️

The premier absolute window for accessing deep mountain trails in Svaneti and Kazbegi. High passes are completely clear of snow, valleys are deep emerald green, and mountain air feels incredibly refreshing.

Avg. Mountain Temp18–26°C
Lowland CitiesHot & sunny
Best ForHigh Caucasus Trekking & Kazbegi Views

The Rtveli Wine Harvest 🍂

Many consider autumn the ultimate peak window. September and October activate Rtveli — the celebratory national grape harvest in Kakheti. Forests turn golden, and weather is perfectly temperate.

Avg. Temp16–25°C
Harvest EnergyAt annual high
Best ForKakheti Grape Harvesting & Culinary Banquets

Powder Slopes & Thermal Luxury ❄️

Winter carpets the Caucasus range in dense powder snow, turning Gudauri and Bakuriani into top-tier ski sanctuaries. Cities get cold, creating the perfect backdrop to escape into steaming sulphur baths.
