Destination Guide

Kazakhstan

The Land of Great Steppes & Modern Marvels

Discover the 9th Largest Country

Heart of Eurasia

Kazakhstan is a country of breathtaking contrasts. From the endless, sweeping steppes where nomadic traditions were born, to the hyper-modern, futuristic skyline of Astana, it offers an adventure unlike any other in Central Asia.

Here, you can hike through alpine meadows, explore dramatic canyons that rival the Grand Canyon, ski down Olympic-grade mountain slopes, and experience the warm hospitality of the Kazakh people in traditional yurts.

Must-Visit Places

Top Destinations

The cultural and financial hub of Kazakhstan, beautifully nestled at the foothills of the Trans-Ili Alatau mountains. It is the perfect blend of a vibrant modern city and accessible alpine nature.

  • Medeu & Shymbulak: The world's highest skating rink and a premium ski resort located just 30 minutes from the city center.
  • Big Almaty Lake: A stunning, turquoise-colored alpine lake situated at 2,511 meters above sea level.
  • Zenkov Cathedral: A gorgeous wooden orthodox cathedral built entirely without nails, located in Panfilov Park.

The futuristic capital rising from the northern steppes. Astana is an architectural playground featuring designs by world-renowned architects.

  • Baiterek Tower: The symbol of the city, representing a mythical tree of life with a golden egg.
  • Khan Shatyr: A massive, transparent tent-like structure that houses a shopping mall and an indoor beach resort.
  • Hazrat Sultan Mosque: One of the largest and most magnificent mosques in Central Asia.

The spiritual and historical heart of Kazakhstan, connected deeply to the ancient Silk Road and Sufi traditions.

  • Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi: A UNESCO World Heritage site built by Amir Timur, showcasing incredible Timurid architecture.
  • Arystan Bab Mausoleum: An important pilgrimage site dedicated to the legendary mentor of Yasawi.
  • Otrar: The ancient ruins of a once-great Silk Road city destroyed by Genghis Khan.

Kazakhstan's untouched nature is its greatest treasure, offering landscapes that look like they belong on another planet.

  • Charyn Canyon: Often compared to the Grand Canyon, featuring the dramatic "Valley of Castles" rock formations.
  • Kolsai & Kaindy Lakes: Stunning mountain lakes. Kaindy is famous for its submerged forest with spruce tree trunks piercing the water surface.
  • Altyn-Emel: Famous for the "Singing Dune" and the multi-colored Aktau mountains.
Climate & Seasons

Best Time to Visit

Kazakhstan has a sharp continental climate. Depending on what you want to experience—from skiing to desert trekking—every season offers something unique.

Spring (April - May)

Ideal Time

Spring is a magical time when the endless steppes burst into color. It is the perfect season for visiting the southern regions (Almaty, Turkestan) before the intense summer heat arrives.

  • Wild tulips bloom across the steppes (Kazakhstan is the ancestral home of the tulip).
  • Comfortable weather for exploring cities and canyons.
  • Celebrate "Nauryz" (Persian New Year) in late March with traditional games and food.

Summer (June - August)

Mountain Trekking

Summer can be quite hot in the cities and steppes (often above 30°C/86°F), making it the perfect time to head into the high mountains of the Tien Shan.

  • Best season for hiking, camping, and horseback riding in the mountains.
  • Visit Kolsai Lakes and the singing dunes of Altyn-Emel.
  • Enjoy the modern air-conditioned marvels of Astana.

Autumn (September - October)

Harvest & Colors

September is arguably the most comfortable month. The heat subsides, and the mountains around Almaty turn into a stunning canvas of gold, red, and orange.

  • Perfect temperatures for sightseeing and outdoor activities.
  • Almaty's famous apple harvest (Almaty literally translates to "Father of Apples").
  • Great time for photography at Charyn Canyon and Big Almaty Lake.

Winter (Dec - February)

Winter Sports

Kazakhstan turns into a winter wonderland. While Astana gets freezing cold, the regions around Almaty offer world-class winter sports facilities.

  • Skiing and snowboarding at Shymbulak Ski Resort.
  • Ice skating at the legendary high-altitude Medeu rink.
  • Experience the true frozen beauty of the steppes (bring very warm clothes!).
