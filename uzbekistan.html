Destination Guide

Uzbekistan

The Heart of the Silk Road

Experience the magic of turquoise domes, ancient cities, and warm hospitality at the crossroads of East and West. Uzbekistan is a treasure trove of architectural marvels and rich cultural heritage that will leave you breathless.

Good to Know

Quick Facts

Currency

Uzbek Som (UZS). Cards are widely accepted in cities; carry some cash for bazaars and rural areas.

Language

Uzbek (official) and Russian widely spoken. All our guides also speak English.

Time Zone

UZT (UTC+5), no daylight saving time observed throughout the year.

Electricity

220V, 50Hz. Type C and F sockets (European two-pin plugs). Bring an adapter if needed.

Must-Visit Places

Top Destinations

Once the capital of Tamerlane's magnificent empire, Samarkand is synonymous with the Silk Road. Its monumental architecture and shimmering blue domes are world-famous.

  • Registan Square: The heart of the ancient city, flanked by three stunning, mosaic-covered madrasahs.
  • Gur-e-Amir: The spectacular mausoleum of Tamerlane, featuring a ribbed azure dome.
  • Shah-i-Zinda: A breathtaking avenue of intricately tiled mausoleums.

With a history spanning over two millennia, Bukhara's Old City is a UNESCO World Heritage site that feels completely untouched by time.

  • Poi Kalyan Ensemble: Featuring the towering Kalon Minaret, which even Genghis Khan spared from destruction.
  • Ark Fortress: The massive, ancient citadel that served as the residence of the Emirs of Bukhara.
  • Trading Domes: Ancient bazaars where you can still buy exquisite carpets, ceramics, and silk.

Stepping into Khiva’s Itchan Kala (inner city) is like stepping directly into an Arabian Nights tale. It is incredibly preserved and compact.

  • Kalta Minor: The iconic, beautifully tiled but unfinished turquoise minaret.
  • Juma Mosque: An incredible 10th-century mosque supported by 212 intricately carved wooden columns.
  • Kuhna Ark: The ruler's fortress offering stunning panoramic views of the entire old city.

The vibrant capital beautifully blends Islamic architecture, Soviet-era monuments, and modern developments, surrounded by wide green avenues.

  • Chorsu Bazaar: A massive, incredibly photogenic domed market offering spices, fruits, and local street food.
  • Tashkent Metro: One of the most beautiful subway systems in the world, with each station featuring unique art and architecture.
  • Khast Imam Complex: Home to one of the world's oldest existing Qurans.
Visa & Entry

Getting In Is Easier Than You Think

Citizens of the United States, Canada, most European Union countries, the United Kingdom, Australia, Japan, and South Korea can enter Uzbekistan visa-free for stays of up to 30 days with just a valid passport — no advance application needed.

If your country isn't on the visa-free list, Uzbekistan's e-visa system lets you apply online and typically receive approval within two business days — no embassy visit required.

Whatever your nationality, just send us a copy of your passport — we'll tell you exactly what (if anything) you need to do before you fly, and help with any paperwork.

Ask About Your Visa
Visa-Free (up to 30-90 days)

EU, UK, USA, Canada, Australia, Japan, South Korea, GCC countries, and most CIS nations.

E-Visa (online, ~2 business days)

Apply via the official e-visa.gov.uz portal — passport, digital photo, and hotel booking required.

Hotel Registration

Foreign visitors must be registered at each place they stay — hotels handle this automatically.

Safety

Uzbekistan is considered one of the safest countries in Central Asia for travelers.

Climate Guide

When to Go

Tashkent has a warm climate with hot summers and mild winters, with rain rare during peak season. Here's what to expect throughout the year.

Best Time of Year ☀️

Spring is the most popular season to visit. Mild days, blooming almond and apricot trees, and green landscapes make this the ideal time for walking tours of Samarkand, Bukhara, and Khiva.

Avg. Temp15–28°C
RainfallModerate
PackLayers

Hot & Dry 🔥

July is the warmest month, with average highs around 36°C and low humidity. Great for clear skies and long sightseeing days, but plan indoor breaks during the midday heat and stay hydrated.

Avg. Temp28–40°C
RainfallAlmost none
PackLight, sun hat

Golden Season 🍂

September and October bring mild, comfortable temperatures between 22–30°C, fresh harvests at local bazaars, and fewer crowds — many consider this the best season alongside spring.

Avg. Temp12–28°C
RainfallLow
PackLight layers

Quiet & Cozy ❄️

January is the coldest month, with highs around 7°C and occasional snow. Tourist sites are nearly empty and hotel prices drop — a great option for budget-conscious, culture-focused travelers who don't mind the chill.

Avg. Temp-2–7°C
RainfallModerate, some snow
PackWarm coat
Logistics

Getting Around Uzbekistan

Flights & Arrival

Most international flights land at Tashkent International Airport (TAS). We arrange a private airport pickup for every guest — just send us your flight details and your driver will be waiting with a name sign.

High-Speed Trains

Uzbekistan operates the region's first high-speed rail network, connecting Tashkent, Samarkand, and Bukhara in just a few comfortable hours. Most of our multi-city tours use these trains between destinations.

Private Transfers

All transfers within cities — to hotels, sites, and between excursions — are included in your tour package with an air-conditioned vehicle and driver.

Before You Pack

Traveler Tips & FAQ

Modest, comfortable clothing covering shoulders and knees is recommended. Comfortable walking shoes are a must — most historic sites involve a fair amount of walking on uneven stone surfaces.
We recommend bottled or filtered water, which is cheap and widely available. Most hotels also provide complimentary bottled water in rooms.
Local SIM cards (Beeline, Ucell, Mobiuz) are inexpensive and easy to get at the airport with your passport. 4G coverage is good in all major cities.
Tipping is appreciated but not obligatory. As a guideline, $5-10 per day for guides and $3-5 per day for drivers is common among international travelers.
Don't miss plov (rice pilaf), shashlik (grilled skewers), samsa (baked pastries), and fresh non bread straight from a tandoor oven. Each region has its own specialties worth seeking out.
